Steep & Sell

Run a coffee cart on a tight budget. Buy stock, dial in a recipe, price every cup, and read the crowd in real time.

How a day works

  1. Check the forecast — weather and a hint about what today's crowd wants.
  2. Buy stock — cups, lids, coffee, milk, sugar. Storage and cash both cap how much you can carry.
  3. Set your recipe — strength, milk and sugar share one fixed cup size, so more of one means less room for another.
  4. Set your price — check it against today's typical local price.
  5. Open the cart — watch customers arrive, adjust price live, read their (specific!) feedback.
  6. Debrief — see what worked, what to fix, and what people are saying about you.
Every run starts with £10.00, minus a £2.00 pitch fee charged every morning before you buy anything — that's a fixed cost regardless of how the day goes. Storage space is fixed too and never grows. Turning some customers away on day one is normal, not a failure. Each day's profit funds the next day's stock and fee.

How to Play

  1. Check the forecast — weather changes footfall; the hint tells you roughly what today's crowd wants.
  2. Buy stock — watch "cups you can make now" vs the expected footfall range. Cash AND storage space both limit you.
  3. Set your recipe — strength/milk/sugar share one 200ml cup. More milk means less room for coffee liquid.
  4. Set price — compare to "typical price nearby." Price too high and people walk; too low and you leave money on the table.
  5. Serve — adjust price live. Read customer comments — they name the exact thing that was wrong.
  6. Debrief — check the insight tip, the buzz feed, and the charts before deciding what to change tomorrow.
